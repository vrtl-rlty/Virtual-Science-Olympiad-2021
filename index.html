What is the lowest and the highest parts of the food pyramid?
In the following map below, we have a food pyramid.
We will start from the bottom of the food pyramid, then make it to the top.

Decomposers

The decomposers are the lowest living thing in the food pyramid. The decomposers will return the nutrients back from the soil.
There are many types of decomposers in different biomes, such as freshwater, forests, and mountains.

Producers

The producers are the 4th living thing on the food pyramid (top to bottom). The producers make, or produce, food for the animals. Producers can also make sugar.

Primary Consumers

The primary consumers are the 3rd living thing on the food pyramid, and the most innocent. The primary consumers will eat the producers, as it needs energy to stay active, and watch out for predators, such as secondary consumers.
More on that next.
The primary consumers most consists of herbivores, such as deer, rabbit, and geese.

Secondary Consumers

The secondary consumers are the 2nd living thing on the food pyramid.
The secondary consumers not only consume the producers, but they consume the primary consumers too.
However, the secondary consumers still get hunted by the top of the food pyramid, Top Level Consumers.
The secondary consumers consists of a mix of carnivores and omnivores.

Top Level Consumers

The top level consumers are the top of the food pyramid, and is the biggeat predator.
These type of consumers are rarely prey, and they consist of mostly carnivores.
